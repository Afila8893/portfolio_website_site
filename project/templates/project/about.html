About National Service Scheme (NSS)

Empowering students through community service since 1969

Introduction

The National Service Scheme (NSS) was introduced in 1969 with the primary objective of developing the personality and character of student youth through voluntary community service.

Rooted in the principle of “Not Me, But You”, NSS encourages students to place community needs above personal interests, aligning closely with the philosophy of Mahatma Gandhi.

Objectives of NSS

  • Understand the community in which they work
  • Identify community problems and contribute to solutions
  • Develop social and civic responsibility
  • Gain skills in mobilizing community participation
  • Acquire leadership qualities and democratic values
  • Promote national integration and social harmony
  • Prepare to face emergencies and natural disasters

Community Participation

Volunteers work directly with local communities through awareness drives, health camps, environment conservation efforts, education support, and more.

Types of NSS Activities

  • Regular Activities: Weekly community-based actions like sanitation, literacy programs, and tree planting.
  • Special Camping: 7-day residential programs focusing on themes like "Healthy Youth for Healthy India", "Clean India", etc.

Our Motto

“Not Me, But You” – reminding every volunteer to put others first and serve selflessly.
